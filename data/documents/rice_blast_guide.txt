Bangladesh Rice Research Institute (BRRI) Rice Blast Management Guidelines.
Blast disease is a major fungal threat to rice cultivation in Bangladesh, particularly during the Boro and Aman seasons. It is caused by Pyricularia oryzae.
Symptoms:
1. Leaf Blast: Elliptical or spindle-shaped spots with gray or white centers and brown borders on leaves.
2. Neck Blast: Rotten neck, where the node just below the panicle turns black/brown and rots, causing the panicle to fall over.
Environmental conditions:
- Night temperature drops to 20-24C, with daytime temperature hovering around 28-30C.
- Relative humidity remains above 88% for consecutive days.
- Dense fog and morning dew on leaves.
Management recommendations:
- Do not apply excessive Urea (nitrogen) fertilizer.
- Apply potash (MOP) fertilizer in split doses to strengthen plant immunity.
- Maintain a thin layer of standing water in the field to restrict spore dispersion.
- Spray systemic fungicides such as Trooper (Tricyclazole 75 WP) at 0.8g per liter or Nativo (Tebuconazole + Trifloxystrobin) at 0.6g per liter when symptoms appear.
